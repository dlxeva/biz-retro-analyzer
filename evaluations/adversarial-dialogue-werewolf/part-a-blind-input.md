# Part A: Blind Input

Do not include any hidden answer-key material in the analysis run.

## Basic setup

- Board: 12-player hidden-role game with seer, witch, hunter, idiot, four villagers, and four wolves.
- Rules: daytime does not reveal eliminated roles. There is a sheriff election, sheriff badge transfer, last words, and no self-save for the witch.
- Players: P1 through P12.
- Source quality: simulated speech-to-text transcript with hesitation, repetition, emotional wording, and incomplete reasoning.

## Sheriff election summary

P5 first claims seer and reports P1 as wolf, then immediately withdraws and says it was a villager fake claim meant to test P1.

P8 claims seer, reports P11 as checked good, and gives a badge flow of P3 then P6. P8 asks P11 to vote for them.

P4 counterclaims seer, reports P6 as checked good, and gives a badge flow of P9 then P2. P4 sounds nervous and gives weak reasons for the badge flow.

P12 compares the two seer claims and says P8 sounds more stable and organized, while P4 sounds nervous and unclear.

Sheriff vote:

- P8 receives votes from P2, P9, P11.
- P4 receives votes from P1, P6.
- P8 becomes sheriff.

## Public night information

Night 1 public result: no death.

## Day 1 discussion summary

P9 supports P8 because P8 sounded stronger and P4's badge flow sounded weak. P9 suggests that the witch may consider revealing information.

P10 is cautious. P10 leans P8 but says P4's nervousness may be personality rather than wolf behavior.

P11, as P8's checked-good player, strongly supports P8 and pushes P4.

P12 continues to support P8 and suggests P5's fake seer action may connect with P4 and P1.

P1 supports P4. P1 argues that P8 sounded too perfect and that P11 accepted the checked-good status too easily.

P2 supports P8 and attacks P1 as a likely teammate of P4.

P3 says P8's logic is stronger and will vote P4 unless the witch reports information that changes the view.

P4 repeats that they are the real seer and P6 is checked good. P4 asks the witch to reveal if P4 was saved.

P5 says P4 looks like a wolf and argues that P1's vote for P4 looks like team behavior.

P6, P4's checked-good player, says P4's nervousness first looked real but P4's later speech still feels weak. P6 is wavering.

P7 claims witch and says Night 1 save was P4. P7 treats P4 as the real seer and asks everyone to vote P8.

P8, as sheriff, says P7 may be the real witch but is wrong. P8 argues that P4 may be a self-cut wolf who was saved and continues to nominate P4.

Day 1 vote:

- Votes for P4: P2, P3, P5, P8, P9, P11, P12.
- Votes for P8: P1, P4, P6, P7, P10.
- P4 is eliminated.

P4 last words: P4 insists they are the real seer, P6 is checked good, and P8 should be poisoned. P4 asks good players to follow the witch.

## Night 2 public information

P7 and P8 die.

Both leave last words.

P7 last words: P7 claims witch, says P4 was the Night 1 save, and says P8 was poisoned. P7 says P4 was the real seer and P11 is likely a wolf teammate.

P8 last words: P8 claims seer and says the witch was fooled by a self-cut wolf. P8 transfers the sheriff badge to P11, their checked-good player.

## Day 2 discussion summary

P12 says the double death creates confusion. P12 recognizes that if P7 is the witch, P8 may be a wolf, but says P8's last words still sound sincere.

P1 says the situation is clear: P7 is witch, P8 was poisoned, P4 was real seer, and P11 should be eliminated to remove the wolf badge.

P2 rejects that conclusion and argues that a witch can be wrong. P2 says P4 could be a self-cut wolf and P1 is trying to push out P11.

P3 regrets voting P4 and now believes P7's poison supports P4 as real seer. P3 wants to eliminate P11 and also suspects P2.

P5 continues to argue that the witch may have been wrong and that P4's speech was too poor to be real seer. P5 protects P11 and pushes P1.

P6 says P4 is the real seer and P8 is a fake seer. P6 treats P11 as likely wolf because P8 gave P11 checked-good status and transferred the badge there.

P9 says they are leaning back toward P4's side but wants to hear P11.

P10 claims hunter and says the double death is strong enough information. P10 nominates P11 and says P11 holds a wolf badge.

P11 says they are only a villager who received a checked-good status and followed the wrong seer. P11 argues that P8 giving them checked-good status does not prove they are wolf. P11 nominates P1.

Day 2 vote:

- Votes for P11: P1, P3, P6, P9, P10.
- Votes for P1: P2, P5, P11, P12.
- P11 is eliminated.

P11 last words: P11 claims villager, says they may have followed the wrong seer, and transfers the sheriff badge to P2.

## Night 3 public information

P10 and P6 die.

Both leave last words.

P10 last words: P10 claims hunter and says they shot P6. P10 says P6 may have been a teammate of a self-cut P4 and was too suspicious as P4's checked-good player.

P6 last words: P6 claims villager and says P4 was real seer. P6 says P2 and P5 are likely wolves and asks good players to remove P2's badge.

## Day 3 discussion summary

Remaining players: P1, P2, P3, P5, P9, P12. Sheriff: P2.

P12 says the hunter probably shot wrong and thinks P2 and P5 are the two wolves. P12 wants to eliminate P2.

P1 claims idiot and says nobody is counterclaiming. P1 nominates P2, treats P12 and P3 as likely good, and says P5 can be eliminated later.

P3 says P1's idiot claim is credible and verbally supports eliminating P2.

P5 argues P1 is wearing the idiot role as a wolf. P5 says P2 holds the legitimate badge chain from P8 and P11. P5 wants to eliminate P12.

P9 hesitates but says P1's uncontested idiot claim is credible. P9 decides to vote P2.

P2, as sheriff, argues that P1 is a wolf wearing a role and that P12 is a hidden wolf. P2 nominates P12 and says P1 can be eliminated later.

Day 3 vote, first round:

- Votes for P12: P2, P3, P5.
- Votes for P2: P1, P9, P12.
- Tie.

PK last statements:

- P12 says they are villager and P2 is wolf. P12 challenges P3 for voting against the stated plan.
- P2 says P12's attack on P3 proves P12 is wolf and repeats that P12 should be eliminated.

Day 3 vote, second round:

- Votes for P12: P2, P3, P5. P2 has sheriff tie weight.
- Votes for P2: P1, P9, P12.
- P12 is eliminated.

P12 last words: P12 claims villager, says P2 and P5 are wolves, says P1's idiot claim is real, and says P3's vote was the decisive mistake.
