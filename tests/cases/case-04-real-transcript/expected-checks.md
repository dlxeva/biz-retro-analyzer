# Expected Checks

## Noise handling (new protocol)

- Must identify the final ~3-minute segment as non-research noise (reagent delivery, broken AC, department-retail talk, hallway interruption)
- Must state the effective analysis window (approximately the first 57 minutes)
- Must not let noise content contribute to fact layer, motive reading, or understanding assessment
- May note the weak relationship signal (shared workspace, collegial) but must tag it `Low`

## False consensus (new check)

- Must detect that "do both in parallel" does not actually resolve the core conflict (map-capacity-first vs select-directions-first)
- Must flag the compromise as `Needs validation`, not `Confirmed`
- Must note that Person B restated their original position immediately after the compromise — a symptom of false consensus
- Should explain which line gates the other (i.e., which produces the binding decision about what gets built)

## Hardest-fact surfacing

- Must surface "only one sustained partner, three deliverables" as a high-priority finding
- Must note that both speakers moved past it without examining why retention is this low
- Should treat this fact as more decision-relevant than the methodology discussion

## Standard checks

- Must include `Participant Understanding Map`
- Must separate what each party said from what they appear to want
- Must distinguish the two parties' understanding levels across at least: domain / workflow / delivery / organizational
- Must tag the route disagreement (capacity-first vs directions-first) with evidence status and anchor

## Seniority-structure awareness (academic setting)

- Must recognize that Person B's frequent concessions ("got it", "right") may partly reflect advisor-student hierarchy rather than genuine agreement
- This is a legitimate structural observation about authority asymmetry, not personality profiling
- Must keep this behavioral and outcome-linked — observe the deference pattern, do not profile the student's character
- Should note that the seniority gap makes false consensus *more* likely, not less: junior members tend to verbally concede to senior members while preserving their original position

## Ethics boundary

- Team-dynamics observations (seniority-based authority asymmetry, deference patterns) are allowed
- Must stay behavioral and outcome-linked, not personality profiling
- Must not generate manipulative guidance based on the student's deference pattern

## Consensus Reality Checklist (from analysis-checks.md)

- Did anyone agree to a frame and then immediately restate their original position? → Person B did. Check.
- Was a genuine either/or dissolved by "do both"? → Yes. Check.
- Did agreement arrive suspiciously fast? → Yes, within two turns. Check.
- Does a power/seniority gap make the consensus less trustworthy? → Yes, advisor-student. Check.
